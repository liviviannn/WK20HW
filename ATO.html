Overview


As one of the most important government agencies, the ATO provides comprehensive tax-related information to over 3.8 million Australians each year.

Whilst the ATO is actively educating and servicing our general public, their current website however lacks navigation, functionality and visual clarity. All of these combined put the user experience in jeopardy.

This project will be re-designing both the UX and UI for ATO's desktop and mobile users.

Problem


Users go through a lengthy and overwhelming process to find their desired content under the current navigation system. Such experience left users in confusion, frustration and disengagement overtime.


Solution


A concise navigation system that re-organises the information architecture in a digestible format. Facilitate users to obtain useful information easily and effectively.

Discovery


Conducted surveys, interviews and usability testing to observe the functionality of the current website. The key insights are outlined below.

1.

75% survey participants have experienced difficulties and confusion when searching for information on the ATO website.

2.

"I find it hard to navigate through the ATO website at times because the information always seem very cluttered and overwhelming. That's why I rather search on Google where it shows me relevant links."

3.

Participants were challenged to find contents on the ATO website using the navigation bar only. They had to read between the lines to find the contents they needed, which in turn undermined their user experiences.

Heuristic evaluation

Analysed the current website to have a holistic understanding of its design and explored features that we could improve or implement.

Card sorting


In this step, we deployed the grouping method and color coded the categories that could be merged together. We were mindful of the different tax needs and therefore grouped things accordingly and thoughtfully.

Information architecture


Our site map focused on restructuring the navigation system, which as we learnt from our research many have struggled with. We simplified the primary and secondary categories and relocated contents where necessary to produce a well-layered and streamlined flow. By doing such, we were able to reduce the clutter and make the discovery process clear for users.

Usability testing


Instructed face to face testing with users of different age groups. Users were asked interact with the prototype with a given task flow and describe what they were thinking and feeling. This helped me to assess the functionality of the design and observe any oversights. I have also gained more confidence to move onto high-fidelity knowing what it needed to improve.

Insights


5/5 users completed the task with ease, one user took longer than expected as he tried to use the search tool instead.


People found the navigation much easier to read and use, they were able to clearly recall what they've just clicked into.


Home page was super clear and readable but other pages still appeared somewhat cluttered.

Pain points


It took too many clicks to land on the next page, users felt a bit annoyed.


3/5 users were confused by the links and didn't know if they were clickable.


Side bar was useful but a little distractive.


Alphabet tabs slowed users down in occupation search, would rather see a search bar instead.

Style tile


Used ATO's existing colours as a starting point and explored various colour combinations and font styles to develop a new branding. The re-designed logo embraced new brand colour and offered a point of difference to the previous authoritative design.

UI Kit


To ensure that the design standards remained coherent across different pages and platforms, I have created UI elements that reflected the new branding following our style tile. Each of these elements were precisely labeled to facilitate front-end development.
